# Cited Sources — Access Guide

**Paper:** *Divine Purpose in Human Suffering: Theodicy and Moral Formation in Job and Mencius*
**Target journal:** *Biblical Interpretation* (Brill)

This guide lists every source cited in the manuscript with (1) the exact citation, (2) an access-status flag, and (3) a lawful route to obtain it. It is provided **in place of** redistributing the source PDFs themselves: most of these works are in copyright, so committing their PDFs to this repository would be infringement, and downloading them in bulk through an institutional login violates publisher license terms. Pull paywalled items **yourself**, one at a time, through Vanderbilt's library for personal research use.

## Status flags

- **OA** — Open access. Free and lawful to download.
- **PD** — Public domain. Free and lawful to download.
- **Paywalled** — In copyright. Obtain via Vanderbilt Library or purchase; do not redistribute.

## General routes

- **Vanderbilt Library discovery:** https://www.library.vanderbilt.edu — primary route for almost everything below, including e-book platforms (JSTOR Books, Project MUSE, Oxford Academic, Cambridge Core, Brill) bundled in Vanderbilt's subscriptions, plus interlibrary loan.
- **WorldCat:** https://search.worldcat.org — find which libraries hold a title; useful for print and ILL.

> Direct URLs appear below **only** where I verified them. For the rest I name the publisher/platform and point to the library, rather than guess deep links that may be wrong.

## Sources (alphabetical, matching the bibliography)

1. **Back, Youngsun.** "Confucian Heaven (天 *Tian*): Moral Economy and Contingency." *European Journal for Philosophy of Religion* 8, no. 1 (2016): 51–77.
   - **OA.** *EJPR* is open access. Read/download free via PhilArchive: https://philarchive.org/rec/BACCHM

2. **Bloom, Irene.** "Mencian Arguments on Human Nature (*Jen-hsing*)." *Philosophy East and West* 44, no. 1 (1994): 19–53.
   - **Paywalled.** University of Hawai'i Press. Via Vanderbilt: JSTOR or Project MUSE (search the title).

3. **Clines, David J. A.** *Job 38–42.* Word Biblical Commentary 18B. Nashville: Thomas Nelson, 2011.
   - **Paywalled.** Word Biblical Commentary (rights now HarperCollins Christian / Zondervan Academic). Via Vanderbilt Library / WorldCat.

4. **Clooney, Francis X.** *Comparative Theology: Deep Learning Across Religious Borders.* Malden, MA: Wiley-Blackwell, 2010.
   - **Paywalled.** Wiley (https://www.wiley.com). Via Vanderbilt Library / WorldCat.

5. **Graham, A. C.** *Disputers of the Tao: Philosophical Argument in Ancient China.* La Salle, IL: Open Court, 1989.
   - **Paywalled.** Open Court (in print). Via Vanderbilt Library / WorldCat.

6. **Habel, Norman C.** *The Book of Job: A Commentary.* Old Testament Library. Philadelphia: Westminster, 1985.
   - **Paywalled.** Westminster John Knox Press. Via Vanderbilt Library / WorldCat.

7. **Ing, Michael D. K.** *The Vulnerability of Integrity in Early Confucian Thought.* New York: Oxford University Press, 2017.
   - **Paywalled.** Oxford University Press; also on Oxford Academic (academic.oup.com), accessible through Vanderbilt's OUP subscription.

8. **Ivanhoe, Philip J.** *Ethics in the Confucian Tradition: The Thought of Mengzi and Wang Yangming.* 2nd ed. Indianapolis: Hackett, 2002.
   - **Paywalled.** Hackett Publishing (https://www.hackettpublishing.com). Via Vanderbilt Library / WorldCat.

9. **Koehler, Ludwig, and Walter Baumgartner.** *The Hebrew and Aramaic Lexicon of the Old Testament* (HALOT). Leiden: Brill, 1994–2000.
   - **Paywalled.** Brill (https://www.brill.com); available as Brill reference works online through Vanderbilt's Brill subscription. WorldCat for print.

10. **Lee, Archie C. C.** "Cross-Textual Hermeneutics and Identity in Multi-Scriptural Asia." In *Christian Theology in Asia*, edited by Sebastian C. H. Kim, 179–204. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2008.
    - **Paywalled.** Cambridge University Press; chapter on Cambridge Core (https://www.cambridge.org/core), accessible through Vanderbilt's CUP subscription. (Chapter 9, pp. 179–204.)

11. **Newsom, Carol A.** *The Book of Job: A Contest of Moral Imaginations.* New York: Oxford University Press, 2003.
    - **Paywalled.** Oxford University Press; also Oxford Academic via Vanderbilt.

12. **Perkins, Franklin.** *Heaven and Earth Are Not Humane: The Problem of Evil in Classical Chinese Philosophy.* Bloomington: Indiana University Press, 2014.
    - **Paywalled.** Indiana University Press: https://iupress.org/9780253011725/heaven-and-earth-are-not-humane/ — also check JSTOR Books / Project MUSE via Vanderbilt.

13. **Seneca, Lucius Annaeus.** *De Providentia.* In *Moral Essays*, vol. 1, translated by John W. Basore. Loeb Classical Library. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1928.
    - **PD.** Latin text is public domain — The Latin Library (https://www.thelatinlibrary.com) and Perseus (https://www.perseus.tufts.edu). Basore's **1928** Loeb English translation entered the US public domain on 1 Jan 2024; search Internet Archive (https://archive.org) for "Seneca Moral Essays Basore 1928." (The current in-print Loeb is a separate, copyrighted edition.)

14. **Seow, Choon-Leong.** *Job 1–21: Interpretation and Commentary.* Illuminations. Grand Rapids: Eerdmans, 2013.
    - **Paywalled.** Eerdmans (https://www.eerdmans.com). Via Vanderbilt Library / WorldCat.

15. **Shun, Kwong-loi.** *Mencius and Early Chinese Thought.* Stanford: Stanford University Press, 1997.
    - **Paywalled.** Stanford University Press (https://www.sup.org). Via Vanderbilt Library / WorldCat.

16. **Sugirtharajah, R. S.** *The Bible and the Third World: Precolonial, Colonial, and Postcolonial Encounters.* Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2001.
    - **Paywalled.** Cambridge University Press; Cambridge Core via Vanderbilt.

17. **Surin, Kenneth.** *Theology and the Problem of Evil.* Oxford: Blackwell, 1986.
    - **Paywalled.** Blackwell (now Wiley); a Wipf & Stock reprint is also available. Via Vanderbilt Library / WorldCat.

18. **Van Norden, Bryan W., trans.** *Mengzi: With Selections from Traditional Commentaries.* Indianapolis: Hackett, 2008.
    - **Paywalled.** Hackett Publishing (https://www.hackettpublishing.com). Via Vanderbilt Library / WorldCat.

19. **Zhu Xi.** *Sishu zhangju jizhu* 四書章句集注 [Collected Commentaries on the Four Books]. Beijing: Zhonghua shuju, 1983.
    - **Paywalled (this edition) / PD (underlying text).** The cited 1983 Zhonghua shuju critical edition is in copyright as a typeset edition — via Vanderbilt Library / WorldCat. The underlying classical text (Zhu Xi, d. 1200) is public domain; the Four Books and many traditional commentaries are freely readable at the Chinese Text Project (https://ctext.org).

## Summary

- **Free now (OA / PD):** Back (2016), Seneca *De Providentia* (Latin + Basore's 1928 translation), and the classical Four Books text underlying Zhu Xi (ctext.org).
- **Everything else:** in copyright — obtain through Vanderbilt's subscriptions or interlibrary loan for personal research use.
