# Cited Sources — Access Guide

**Paper:** *Divine Purpose in Human Suffering: Theodicy and Moral Formation in Job and Mencius*
**Target journal:** *Biblical Interpretation* (Brill)

This guide lists every source cited in the manuscript with (1) the exact citation, (2) an access-status flag, (3) a lawful route to obtain it, (4) the **specific section** it is cited for, and (5) **Google Scholar / Google Books** links to that work. It is provided **in place of** redistributing the source PDFs themselves: most of these works are in copyright, so committing their PDFs to this repository would be infringement, and downloading them in bulk through an institutional login violates publisher license terms. Pull paywalled items **yourself**, one at a time, through Vanderbilt's library for personal research use.

## Status flags

- **OA** — Open access. Free and lawful to download.
- **PD** — Public domain. Free and lawful to download.
- **Paywalled** — In copyright. Obtain via Vanderbilt Library or purchase; do not redistribute.

## General routes

- **Vanderbilt Library discovery:** https://www.library.vanderbilt.edu — primary route for almost everything below, including e-book platforms (JSTOR Books, Project MUSE, Oxford Academic, Cambridge Core, Brill) bundled in Vanderbilt's subscriptions, plus interlibrary loan.
- **WorldCat:** https://search.worldcat.org — find which libraries hold a title; useful for print and ILL.

## About the Scholar / Books links

The **Scholar** and **Books** links below are *search-query* links: they always resolve, and they land you on the work's record (Google Scholar — often with an "All versions" list that includes free PDFs) or its in-book preview (Google Books, where the publisher allows one). I have **not** fabricated page-anchored deep links (`books.google.com/books?id=…&pg=PA…`), because those require per-book volume IDs I can't verify and would likely break. Instead, each entry gives the **exact cited page(s)** — open the preview and jump to those pages, or paste the page-relevant phrase into Google Books' "Search inside" box.

> Verified full-text/publisher URLs (e.g., Back on PhilArchive, Perkins at Indiana UP) are shown explicitly where I confirmed them.
